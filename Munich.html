Nymphenburg Palace is located near the city center in Munich. This Baroque style palace was originally built as a summer residence to celebrate the birth of the Elector's son. The stunning palace boasts a large french style garden, and gondolas rides are available to ride on the central canal. Every new family to inhabit the Nymphenburg has expanded it by a few rooms.

Neue Pinakothek is a beautiful art museum located in a new postmodern building. It displays primarily European art of the 18th and 19th century, including renowned names such as, Monet and Van Gogh. Under supervision of the Bavarian State Painting Collections which houses an expanded collection of more than 3.000 European paintings from classicism to art nouveau.

St. Peter's Church is a stunning Roman Catholic church in the city center of Munich. While the interior architecture is unparalleled in its beauty, the most exciting part of this site is its tower. A climb up 299 steps, awards the visitor with one of the most captive views of Munich.

Hofbräuhaus is a lively and fun indoor beer garden of sorts. Servers dressed in traditional Bavarian clothes add to the authenticity and the food and live band are equally enjoyable! Come and hang out any time of day and enjoy some local German beers too.
