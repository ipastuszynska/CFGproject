Island Hopping is very easy to do once in the Canary Islands, especially since Gran Canaria, as the capital, is so well-connected. There are seven islands in total: Gran Canaria, Tenerife, La Palma, Fuerte Ventura, La Gomera, El Hierro, and Lanzarote. These islands boast everything from beautiful mountains to stunning beaches. Traveliing by boat is the better option, because you can bring along your car.

Playa del Ingles is a sea resort located on the south coast of the island. It is a popular destination for travellers because of the many seaside hotels and bustling restaurants in the area. However, the beach specifically, is the main attraction. The 3 kms of white sands and sparkling blue water cater to everyone from honeymorroners to wqater sports enthusiasts.

St. Peter's Church is a stunning Roman Catholic church in the city center of Munich. While the interior architecture is unparalleled in its beauty, the most exciting part of this site is its tower. A climb up 299 steps, awards the visitor with one of the most captive views of Munich.

Hofbräuhaus is a lively and fun indoor beer garden of sorts. Servers dressed in traditional Bavarian clothes add to the authenticity. Equally enjoyable, is the live band playing right in the center of the venue, allowing for some great photo opportunities. Come and hang out any time of day and enjoy some local German beers too!

Wirtshaus in der Au is a lovely looking Renaissance house offering traditional Bavarian cuisine. Located minutes formt the city center, this bustling restaurant is a fantastic option for a lunch break frrom sightseeing around the city. Be sure to make a reservation first though, the friendly staff and environment make this place an always busy one!

Cotidiano is a breakfast, lunch, and brunch kind of place with the option of al fresco seating when the weather is nice. While the traaditional options are available on the menu, tis restaurant also has a selection of around the breakfasts for those who are adventurous enough to try!
